# Game Concept

---

## Elevator Pitch

> *Place gears. Route steam. Make it spin.*

---

## High-Level Pitch

Steamheart is a steampunk machine-building puzzle game where the player assembles contraptions from a live feed of incoming parts, placing them on a grid to route motion from a power source to a target mechanism. Each level presents a partially-complete machine inside a brass-and-copper workshop frame. Gears must mesh, pipes must connect, valves must open in the right order. When the machine is activated, it either roars to life in a cascade of spinning parts, hissing steam, and thumping pistons — or it jams, overloads, and fails spectacularly. The satisfaction is entirely in that moment of activation: watching something you built actually work.

---

## Genre Definition

- **Primary genre:** Puzzle / Arcade
- **Secondary influences:** Factory/builder games, Pipe Dream, Zachtronics-lite
- **Closest references (mood, not mechanics):** Opus Magnum, Little Inferno, Cogmation
- **Grid-based, not physics-based:** No free-form simulation; rules are discrete and deterministic

---

## Player Fantasy

The player is a steampunk engineer in a cluttered workshop. They don't fully control what arrives — the machine feeds you parts from a queue — but they control where everything goes. The fantasy is:

- *I built this machine. I figured out the puzzle. I made it work.*
- Watching the assembled machine activate and run is the direct reward for solving the puzzle.
- Failure should also feel expressive — a jam, a burst pipe, an overloaded boiler.

The game rewards spatial thinking, improvisation with constrained materials, and patience.

---

## Why This Fits the Jam Theme

The theme is **"Machines"** — this game is entirely *about* machines. Not incidentally themed around them, but mechanically centered on them:

- The core interaction is building a physical machine
- The win condition is making a machine function
- The visual language is gears, pipes, steam, and metal
- The moment-to-moment feel is the behavior of a working (or failing) machine

This is a thematically solid entry. The concept is immediately readable as "a game about Machines."

---

## What Makes It Appealing / Memorable

| Quality | Why it works |
|---|---|
| **Clear visual payoff** | The machine activating is a satisfying, screenshot-worthy moment |
| **Readable theme** | Steampunk machine-building — nobody needs an explanation |
| **Tactile feel** | Gears spin, pipes hiss, gauges move — the machine feels alive |
| **Puzzle + pressure** | Queue feed creates time/resource pressure without being purely arcade |
| **Short sessions** | Each level is self-contained, ~2–5 minutes — good for jam judges |
| **Aesthetic identity** | Strong steampunk style is distinct and consistent |

---

## Tone and Mood

- Tinkery, slightly whimsical, earnestly industrial
- Not comedic, not grim — it's the joy of a thing that works
- Visual tone: worn brass, copper pipes, riveted steel, billowing steam, amber gauges
- Audio tone: mechanical rhythms, hissing, clanking, the satisfying thrum of a running engine
